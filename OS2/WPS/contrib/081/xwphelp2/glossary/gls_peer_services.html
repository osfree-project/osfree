Typically, the following &link_peer; services will be started on your system:
  • The REQUESTER service allows your computer to see other resources on the LAN. The "requester" is a synonym for "client", really. After the requester has been started, you can access remote files and printers on the LAN. In addition, you can assign directories that are shared by other computers to a driver letter on your own system.

  • The PEER service is a stripped-down version of the server services that are only part of server versions of &os2;. After the peer service has started, your computer can share local directories, files, and printers with other computers on the LAN.

    This service requires that the REQUESTER service is active too.

Use a command prompt for dealing with services in detail:
  • To start a service explicitly, use NET START service, for example, NET START REQUESTER.

  • To stop a service explicitly, use NET STOP service, for example, NET STOP REQUESTER.

  • To find out which services are currently running, type NET START, without a service name.
