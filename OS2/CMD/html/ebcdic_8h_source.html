11/*---------------------------------------------------------------------------
12
13 ebcdic.h
14
15 The CECP 1047 (Extended de-facto EBCDIC) <-> ISO 8859-1 conversion tables,
16 from ftp://aix1.segi.ulg.ac.be/pub/docs/iso8859/iso8859.networking
17
18 NOTES:
19 <Paul_von_Behren@stortek.com> (OS/390 port 12/97)
20 These table no longer represent the standard mappings (for example in the
21 OS/390 iconv utility). In order to follow current standards I remapped
22 ebcdic x0a to ascii x15 and
23 ebcdic x85 to ascii x25 (and vice-versa)
24 Without these changes, newlines in auto-convert text files appeared
25 as literal \045.
26 I'm not sure what effect this remap would have on the MVS and CMS ports, so
27 I ifdef'd these changes. Hopefully these ifdef's can be removed when the
28 MVS/CMS folks test the new mappings.
29
30 Christian Spieler <spieler@ikp.tu-darmstadt.de>, 27-Apr-1998
31 The problem mentioned by Paul von Behren was already observed previously
32 on VM/CMS, during the preparation of the CMS&MVS port of UnZip 5.20 in
33 1996. At that point, the ebcdic tables were not changed since they seemed
34 to be an adopted standard (to my knowledge, these tables are still used
35 as presented in mainfraime KERMIT). Instead, the "end-of-line" conversion
36 feature of Zip's and UnZip's "text-translation" mode was used to force
37 correct mappings between ASCII and EBCDIC newline markers.
38 Before interchanging the ASCII mappings of the EBCDIC control characters
39 "NL" 0x25 and "LF" 0x15 according to the OS/390 setting, we have to
40 make sure that EBCDIC 0x15 is never used as line termination.
41
42 ---------------------------------------------------------------------------*/
